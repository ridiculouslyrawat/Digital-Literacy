## **Stay Safe Online: A Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students**



#### **Financial \& UPI Safety:**



1. Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Your PIN is strictly for sending money or checking your account balance.

2. Double-check the receiver's UPI ID and verified name before confirming any payment, especially when buying used books or gadgets from unknown sellers online.



#### **General Online Safety:**



3\. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all critical accounts, including your college email, banking apps, and primary social media profiles.



4\. Avoid making financial transactions or logging into sensitive accounts while connected to free, public campus or cafe Wi-Fi. 



5\. Be highly skeptical of unsolicited messages or emails offering guaranteed internships, remote jobs, or prize money that require an upfront "registration fee."



6\. Keep your laptop and smartphone operating systems, browsers, and antivirus software updated to patch vulnerabilities against malware.



7\. Audit your social media privacy settings. Ensure your real-time location, personal phone number, and daily schedule are not publicly visible to strangers.



8\. Do not click on suspicious links sent via SMS or WhatsApp (even if they claim to be a package delivery update or a message from a friend) without verifying the source first.



#### **Emergency Reporting Channels:**



If you or a batchmate ever experience financial fraud or cyber harassment, report it immediately:

* National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in \[cite: 84, 121]
* National Cyber Crime Helpline: Dial 1930 (Available 24x7) \[cite: 84, 121]
